General Guidelines For All The Events

1. Entry for school students is FREE. ( You must carry your school ID cards). There is a registration fee for college in the "Register as college" section in this page.
2. All the participants are requested to report at the REGISTRATION DESK on entering the college premises and collect their participation card positively which will also be their entry pass for both the days .
3. Students not participating in any events(Except SIEM Students) may collect their Entry passes from the registration desk by paying the entry fee.
4. On the spot registrations will be closed half an hour before the event begins, as per the schedule provided.
5. Participants are requested to report atleast half an hour before each event at the event venue.
6. All participants should take a note of the rules and regulations of the events they are participating in from our official website www.siemalacrity.in .
7. Organizers reserve the right to admission on disciplinary grounds and to change or cancel any event if the minimum required number of teams/participants is not met.
8. Student must carry their valid College ID Card without which they will not be allowed to enter the college premises.

Register As College

1. The registration fees for the colleges is ₹500. The registration fees must be paid at the registration desk by the college representative on arrival at the SIEM campus.
2.. By paying this fee, Participants of a particular college can participate in all events except Rock Band, Gully Cricket and Dota 2.
3. For participating in Rock Band, Gully Cricket and Dota 2 separate registration fees for each event needs to be paid. The participants need not to pay the entry fees if they are only participating in these events. The registration fees of these three events are mentioned in the rules of the events section.

Wave Whizz

A waveform is the shape and form of a signal such as a wave moving in a physical medium or an abstract representation. A waveform is a set graph that represents an audio signal or recording which shows the changes in amplitude over a certain period of time. The amplitude of the signal is measured on the y-axis (vertical), as time is measured below on the x-axis (horizontal).In this Participants are asked to study a waveform and generate it on a CRO. The waveforms will be elementary.

Bridge It

CIVIL ENGINEERS are born to build and every structure in this world is the gift of civil engineering,since from ancient time to this 21st century. One after another, skills with your knowledge comes out with every new generation and we use it for our benefit, so this is the time to come up with your skills along with the knowledge of yours to make something innovative or somewhat modified structure of the existing ones.

VIEW RULES AND REGISTER

Bridge It Rules

1. Maximum of four members are allowed in each group.
2. Bridge should be at least 650mm and maximum 1000mm long (clear span) and 200mm high.
3. Bridge must be no wider than 150mm. The weight of the bridge should not be more than 500 grams.
4. The width of each support at each end is 20mm(horizontal support) with balsa wood / popsicle sticks (6mm x 12mm x1000mm).
5. It should take minimum weight of 1 kg.
6. Materials to be used are:-
   i. ) Balsa wood sticks/ Popsicle sticks (6mm x 12mm x1000mm) thick and you may take length as per requirement.
   ii. ) 1 tube of quick drying epoxy glue.
   iii. ) 1 piece of 5m long string to tie (if required).
   7. No extra string will be provided apart from mentioned length of string.
8. The bridge will be loaded at the centre, so the wooden pieces must have enough space both vertically and horizontally so that the load hanger can be hanged well.
9. While testing the bridge the load will be increased and it is tested to its extreme strength.
10. Vertical support will be provided by us to hold the bridge at both ends.

Mechanical Engineering Events

Robo Soccer

Soccer is no more bound to the human domain, Robots have crept into it.” Distilling the World’s most popular sport into the field of robotics isn’t an easy task. Problem Statement: The event is simple yet requires crunch handling. Participants are supposed to design a manually controlled bot which can push or kick a medium sized ball. The prime motive is to kick the ball in the opponent’s goalpost and save the ball from going into yours. The bot with more goals will win the game. Skills speed and reflexes of both the bot and the participants will be tested.

VIEW RULES AND REGISTER

Robo Soccer Rules

1. Maximum weight of the machines should be below 6 kg.
2. Maximum dimension of the bot will be 30cm X 30cm X 30cm..
3. The potential difference between any two points should not exceed 12 V at any point of time during the match.
4. The bot can be wired or wireless.
5. Wireless bots should have at least two or more operating frequencies to avoid any frequency interference.
6. 12V DC supply will be provided by us. You will get two long wires (+VE) and (GND).Make your Remote Control setup accordingly, so that you can screw the two wires with your controller in a short time.
7. Controlling wires should be bound into a single strip and should be long in order to avoid any type of wire-crossing with the opponent during the match.
8. Weapons should be safe in its use. No chemicals, fluid and blasting weapons can be used.
9. The part of the bot which can touch the arena is only the “wheels”. No other actuators/parts can touch the arena.
10. Co-coordinators have the right to disqualify any bot which they feel is unsafe for the audience or violating the rules.
11. Readymade base of machine would not be allowed except for wheels, foam, light wooden, thermocol, motor, battery, RF remotes, switches, wires. Rules same for both:
   · The machine should float in arena and no short cuts allowed.   
· The machine should start to run from the starting point of arena.
· The machine should not damage the arena else the team will be disqualified.   
   · The machine can transform its size at the arena but cannot divide in parts.
12. The first prize for this event is Rs 2000/- and second prize is Rs 1000/-
